    Which government department do you think will use and provide evidence
    some government departments that may benefit from the data?

    The Federal and State government can use this data as an administrative record as well
    as assist in being aware of the Indigenous health and welfare in Australia.
    Government can integrate ways to address the gaps in health and
    welfare through monitoring this information over time.

    E.g. provides a record of where Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander lived in Major Cities,
    Inner Regional, Outer Regional or Remote Australia and any changes over time.

    The Australian Institute of Health and Welfare are aiming to close the gap in disadvantage
    by promoting careers in health information so they can utilise this data to know
    the best areas to target their recruitment.
    Source: http://www.aihw.gov.au/indigenous-health-information-workforce/

    The Australian Institute of Criminology also utilises this data in
    examining the disparity between crime rates in certain areas to the
    relevance of the number of Indigenous people living in the area.
    Source: http://www.aic.gov.au/media_library/publications/tandi_pdf/tandi390.pdf
